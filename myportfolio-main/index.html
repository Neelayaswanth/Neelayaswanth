Wind Energy

B.Tech First Year Project
3.5/5

Wind energy is a renewable source of power generated by converting the kinetic energy of wind into electricity using wind turbines. The project likely covered two types of turbines: horizontal-axis wind turbines (HAWT), which are the most efficient, and vertical-axis wind turbines (VAWT), which are less common. Key components of a wind turbine include the rotor blades that capture wind energy, the nacelle housing the gearbox and generator, the tower for height, and the foundation for stability. The process involves wind turning the rotor, which then spins a generator to produce electricity, later transformed to higher voltages for transmission. Factors influencing wind energy include wind speed, direction, and the location of the turbines, with optimal sites being open areas or offshore locations where winds are stronger. Wind energy offers advantages like being clean, renewable, and reducing reliance on fossil fuels, though challenges such as inconsistent power generation, high upfront costs, and visual impact persist. Wind energy can be harnessed at large wind farms or through smaller, localized turbines.
